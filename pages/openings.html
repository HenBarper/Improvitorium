Openings

This page is dedicated to set openings for Harolds or other long forms.

  • Invocation

Invocation

Description

  • A one word suggestion is gotten from the audience, usually an object.
  • The cast "summons" the object with a specifically formatted incantation
  • In each phase cast members step out, point towards the imagined object center stage, or use another manner of taking focus before delivering their line of the incantation.
  • About 10-20 ideas(2-3 per cast member) should be shared in each phase except the final phase.
  • IT IS
    • In the it is phase the object is treated as a person.
    • Cast members state
  • YOU ARE
    • In the you are phase the object is treated as a thing.
    • Cast members talk directly to the object giving it a personal nature or human characteristics.
    • Add on to other cast members ideas from the previous phase to help establish group mind and help memerable ideas rise to the top.
  • THOU ART
    • In the thou art phase the object is treated as a god.
    • To endow the object with godlike qualities the cast members should deliver their lines with the energy and gravitas of a shakespearean actor.
    • The cast members are essentially worshiping the object now.
    • The cast members should return to ideas from the preious phases abstracting and distilling them for the upcoming scenes.
  • I AM
    • In the I am phase cast members become the object.
    • Each cast member should speak only once in this phase.
    • Don't introduce any new ideas in this phase.
    • Ideally the cast is creating a list of their best ideas here at the end
    • Once every cast member has said their I am line, they all say together "I am (the obect)!"

Example

  • Suggestion: Can
  • IT IS
    • Improviser: It is a siren for my cat.
    • Improviser: It is sturdy when full and fragile when empty.
    • Improviser: It is modern and antique.
    • 7-17 more...
  • YOU ARE
    • Improviser: Your cheapness is under appreciated.
    • Improviser: You are dependable.
    • Improviser: You are full of stinky cat food or delicious soup.
    • 7-17 more...
  • THOU ART
    • Improviser: Thou art an endless boon whose importance humanity fails to realize!
    • Improviser: Thou art that which contains boundless pleasures for all life forms of the earth!
    • Improviser: Thou art a savior in some capacity no matter your physical condition.
    • 7-17 more...
  • I AM
    • I am an undecerning helper!
    • I am a thankless provider!
    • I am a broken savior!
    • Go until each cast member has had a turn
    • (All in unison)I AM CAN!

Purpose

  • This opening is great at allowing a team to generate group mind and build up ideas collaboratively.
  • The theatricality is generally engaging to an audience.

Sources

  • The Upright Citizens Brigade Comedy Improv Manual (second edition) by Matt Besser, Ian Roberts, and Matt Walsh - p. 241
